What Is Hagia Sophia?

If you visit only one historical attraction in Istanbul, make it Hagia Sophia.

For nearly 1,500 years, this extraordinary building has been one of the world's most important religious and architectural landmarks.

It has served as a Byzantine cathedral, an Ottoman mosque, a museum, and a mosque once again. Today, Hagia Sophia remains one of the most visited attractions in Turkiye.

What makes it special is not only its size or beauty. It is the unique combination of Byzantine and Ottoman history under one roof.

Inside Hagia Sophia

What You'll See Inside Hagia Sophia

Focus on the features that make the visit feel unforgettable, even if you only have one or two hours.

The Famous Dome

The giant dome is the first thing most visitors notice. Completed in 537 AD, it was considered one of the world's great engineering achievements.

Byzantine Mosaics

Christian mosaics and Islamic elements tell the story of Istanbul's changing history under one roof.

Ottoman Calligraphy

Massive circular calligraphy panels dominate the interior and remain among the largest examples of Islamic calligraphy.

Upper Gallery

The upper gallery offers some of the best interior views and is often the highlight of the experience.

Combine Your Visit

What can you combine with Hagia Sophia?

Hagia Sophia sits in the heart of Sultanahmet, so it is easy to pair it with nearby landmarks on the same day.

Hagia Sophia + Blue Mosque

The easiest combination. They are only a few minutes apart and show two very different layers of Istanbul's religious architecture.

Is Hagia Sophia Worth Visiting?

Short answer: absolutely. Even travelers who are not especially interested in religion or history are often impressed by the scale of the building.

Very few places in the world combine Byzantine architecture, Ottoman heritage, religious significance and UNESCO-level history in one location.

Hagia Sophia Dress Code

Because Hagia Sophia functions as a mosque, visitors should dress respectfully.

  • Women should cover hair, shoulders and knees.
  • Men should avoid sleeveless tops and shorts above the knee.
  • Scarves are usually available near the entrance, but bringing your own is safer.

Hagia Sophia Opening Hours

As of June 2026, the upper-gallery tourist route generally operates from 08:00 to 19:00 between April and October, and from 09:00 to 19:00 between November and March. Last admission is normally around 18:30.

On Fridays, tourist access closes from approximately 12:00 to 14:30 for congregational prayer. Religious events and restoration work can still cause same-day changes.

Do tourists need a ticket for Hagia Sophia?

Yes. Cultural visitors use the paid upper-gallery route, which costs €25 on site as of June 2026. The ground-floor prayer area is reserved for worship.

What does the tourist ticket include?

The standard ticket covers the upper-gallery visitor route and important Byzantine mosaics. The online ticket offered here starts from €28 and adds advance booking, mobile confirmation, audio commentary and AR features.

Can children enter free?

Children under 8 enter the upper-gallery route free when an adult presents their passport or ID.

Does Museum Pass Istanbul include Hagia Sophia?

No. Museum Pass Istanbul does not cover the standard foreign-visitor upper-gallery ticket. Türkiye-issued MüzeKart arrangements are separate.

Can I skip every line?

The online ticket skips the ticket-office line. All visitors must still pass mandatory security screening.
