Is Hagia Sophia Worth Visiting in 2026?

⏱ 6 min read✦ Local expert guideUpdated 2026

Quick answer

Yes. If you are visiting Istanbul for the first time, Hagia Sophia is one of the few attractions that deserves a place on every itinerary.

Many famous attractions around the world are beautiful but often fail to meet expectations. Hagia Sophia is not one of them. It is one of those rare places where the reality feels even more impressive than the photographs. Even visitors who are not particularly interested in history often leave impressed by the sheer scale of the building.

Why is Hagia Sophia so famous?

Hagia Sophia has stood in the heart of Istanbul for nearly 1,500 years. Completed in 537 AD during the reign of Byzantine Emperor Justinian I, it remained the largest cathedral in the world for almost a thousand years. After the Ottoman conquest of Constantinople in 1453 it was converted into a mosque, in 1935 it became a museum, and today it functions as a mosque again while remaining one of the most visited landmarks in Türkiye.

What makes Hagia Sophia unique is that it combines Byzantine Christianity and Ottoman Islam in a single building. Visitors can see Byzantine mosaics, Ottoman calligraphy, marble columns from ancient civilizations, massive Islamic medallions and Christian architectural elements all under one roof. Few monuments in the world tell so many different stories at once.

What will you actually see inside?

Many visitors arrive expecting a museum full of exhibits and artifacts. That is not what Hagia Sophia is — the building itself is the attraction. The moment you enter, your eyes immediately move upward. The enormous central dome dominates the entire interior, and around it are layers of architectural details that reveal how different civilizations adapted the building over the centuries.

The central dome

The dome remains one of the greatest engineering achievements of the ancient world. Even today, many visitors struggle to understand how it was built nearly fifteen centuries ago.

Byzantine mosaics

The upper gallery contains some of the most important surviving Byzantine mosaics. These artworks provide a rare glimpse into the Christian history of the building.

Giant Ottoman calligraphy

The enormous circular medallions hanging inside Hagia Sophia are among the largest examples of Islamic calligraphy in the world.

The Weeping Column

One of the most famous features inside Hagia Sophia. Visitors traditionally place a thumb inside the opening and make a wish.

Is the upper gallery worth it?

This is probably the most common question visitors ask, and our answer is yes. The upper gallery gives you better views of the dome, access to the Byzantine mosaics, fewer crowds and better photography opportunities. Visitors who skip the gallery often leave without fully understanding why Hagia Sophia became famous in the first place. If your budget allows it, the gallery ticket is worth the extra cost.

How long do you need?

Visit type Time needed
Quick visit 45–60 minutes
Comfortable visit 90 minutes
History enthusiasts 2 hours or more

For most travellers, around 90 minutes is ideal. That gives you enough time to explore the gallery, take photographs and appreciate the architecture without feeling rushed.

Best time to visit Hagia Sophia

This is where local knowledge becomes valuable. Many tourists arrive between 11:00 AM and 3:00 PM — unfortunately, this is also when Hagia Sophia is at its busiest, as large tour groups, cruise passengers and day visitors all arrive around the same time.

If possible, visit before 9:00 AM, when the atmosphere is quieter, the light through the windows is beautiful and photography is easier. After 4:30 PM also works well, once many tour groups have left and lines are shorter. Try to avoid Friday midday, when Friday prayers attract large numbers of worshippers, access may be restricted and crowds are significantly larger.

Common mistakes visitors make

Arriving at noon is by far the biggest mistake — many visitors end up spending more time in crowds than actually enjoying the monument. Ignoring the dress code is another: because Hagia Sophia functions as a mosque, visitors should dress respectfully, women should bring a scarf, and shoulders and knees should be covered. Skipping the upper gallery means missing some of the building's most fascinating details. And visiting without understanding the history sells the experience short — a few minutes of reading beforehand makes the building far more meaningful.

Who should visit Hagia Sophia?

Hagia Sophia is ideal for first-time visitors, history lovers, architecture enthusiasts, photographers, families and cultural travellers. It may be less interesting for travellers who prefer interactive attractions or modern entertainment — but most visitors find it impressive regardless of their interest in history.

Local tip. If you only have one morning in Sultanahmet, follow this route: Hagia Sophia at opening time, then the Blue Mosque, then the Basilica Cistern, then lunch nearby. This avoids the largest crowds and lets you see Istanbul's most famous attractions efficiently.

FAQ

Is Hagia Sophia free?

The prayer area is free. Some visitor areas require a ticket.

Can tourists still visit Hagia Sophia?

Yes. Tourists are welcome outside prayer restrictions.

Is the upper gallery worth paying for?

For most visitors, yes. It offers the best views and access to important mosaics.

How long does a visit take?

Most people spend between 60 and 90 minutes.

What should I wear?

Modest clothing that covers shoulders and knees. Women should carry a scarf.

Final verdict

If you only visit one historical monument in Istanbul, make it Hagia Sophia. The Blue Mosque is beautiful, Topkapi Palace is fascinating and the Basilica Cistern is unforgettable — but Hagia Sophia is the place that best represents Istanbul itself, where Byzantine and Ottoman history meet beneath one extraordinary dome.
