Hagia Sophia vs Blue Mosque: Which Should You Visit?

⏱ 5 min read✦ Local expert guideUpdated 2026

Good news: you do not need to choose between Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque. They are only a few minutes apart, but they offer very different experiences.

Quick answer

Visit Hagia Sophia first, then walk directly to the Blue Mosque. Hagia Sophia is usually the bigger historical experience; the Blue Mosque is calmer and more purely Ottoman.

Hagia Sophia

Hagia Sophia is best for history lovers, architecture enthusiasts and anyone interested in Byzantine heritage. Highlights include the massive dome, Byzantine mosaics, Ottoman calligraphy and the upper gallery.

Blue Mosque

The Blue Mosque is best for visitors who want an active religious atmosphere, traditional Ottoman architecture and beautiful exterior photography. Highlights include blue Iznik tiles, six minarets and the large courtyard.

Which One Is More Impressive?

Most first-time visitors choose Hagia Sophia. Its scale is difficult to compare with any other building in Istanbul, and the layered Byzantine-Ottoman story feels unique.

Best Visiting Order

Visit Hagia Sophia first. Then walk directly across Sultanahmet Square to the Blue Mosque. This is the easiest route and helps you manage the busier attraction early.

Local Tip

Local tip. Use the park between the two buildings for photos. It gives you a clear sense of how close they are and captures one of Istanbul's most famous views.
