Basilica Cistern Ticket & Visitor Guide

From €57.47English guided tourUpdated July 2026

The Basilica Cistern is one of the easiest and most rewarding add-ons after Hagia Sophia: it is underground, atmospheric, close to Sultanahmet Square and usually takes less than an hour.

Quick answer

The guided fast-track Basilica Cistern ticket featured here starts from €57.47 and includes English guidance. The official daytime foreign visitor ticket is listed at 1,950 TL, so the online guided ticket costs more because it adds guided context and fast-track convenience.

What this Basilica Cistern ticket includes

This ticket is best positioned as a guided experience, not only a basic entrance ticket. That matters because many visitors walk through the cistern quickly and miss the stories behind the columns, the water system and the Medusa heads.

  • Fast-track Basilica Cistern entry where stated by the ticket provider.
  • English guided tour.
  • Practical help understanding what you are seeing underground.
  • Easy add-on before or after Hagia Sophia.

Official price vs guided ticket price

The official Basilica Cistern daytime foreign visitor ticket is listed at 1,950 TL. The guided fast-track ticket offered here starts from €57.47. That difference is important to explain clearly: the guided ticket is not the cheapest possible entry, but it adds a guide and a smoother visitor experience.

Opening hours

The official visitor information lists the Basilica Cistern as open every day from 09:00 to 22:00. The regular daytime ticket window is listed as 09:00-18:30, with a separate Night Shift ticket from 19:30-22:00.

What you will see inside

Inside the Basilica Cistern, look for the long rows of columns, reflected water, changing light, the Column of Tears and the famous Medusa heads. The atmosphere is very different from Hagia Sophia: darker, cooler and much more cinematic.

Best route from Hagia Sophia

Walk from Hagia Sophia toward Sultanahmet Square and follow signs toward Yerebatan Caddesi. The walk usually takes only a few minutes, which is why Hagia Sophia + Basilica Cistern is one of the strongest same-day combinations in the Old City.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Basilica Cistern worth visiting?

Yes, especially if you are already visiting Hagia Sophia. It is close, visually unique and takes less time than most major museums.

Is this the cheapest Basilica Cistern ticket?

No. It is a guided fast-track ticket. The official daytime foreign visitor ticket is listed at 1,950 TL, while this guided ticket starts from €57.47.

How long should I spend inside?

Most visitors spend 30 to 60 minutes.

Can I combine Basilica Cistern with Hagia Sophia?

Yes. They are only a short walk apart and work very well in the same Sultanahmet route.
