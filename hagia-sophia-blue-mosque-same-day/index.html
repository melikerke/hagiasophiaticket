Hagia Sophia And Blue Mosque Same Day Plan

⏱ 6 min read✦ Visitor planningUpdated 2026

Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque stand only a few minutes apart, so visiting both on the same day is one of the easiest and most rewarding plans in Istanbul.

Quick answer

Visit Hagia Sophia first if it is your priority, then walk to the Blue Mosque. Add Basilica Cistern before or after depending on queues, prayer times and how much time you have.

Best same-day order

The two landmarks are close, but both are active religious sites. Timing matters more than distance.

Can You Visit Both In One Day?

Yes. Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque are across Sultanahmet Square from each other, so the walk between them takes only a few minutes.

The real planning issue is not distance; it is prayer times, queues, security and how much time you want inside each place.

Best Order: Hagia Sophia First

For most first-time visitors, Hagia Sophia should come first because the upper-gallery route, mosaics and ticket process need more planning.

The Blue Mosque is free to enter, but tourist access also pauses around prayer times.

Simple Morning Plan

Start with Hagia Sophia near opening time, then cross Sultanahmet Square to the Blue Mosque once you have finished the upper gallery.

If the Blue Mosque is temporarily closed for prayer, use that time for exterior photos or Basilica Cistern.

Simple Afternoon Plan

If you arrive later, check prayer timing first. Hagia Sophia after the midday rush can work well, especially outside Friday access pauses.

Late afternoon light is also excellent for exterior photos of both landmarks.

What To Wear For Both Mosques

Dress modestly for both sites. Women should be ready to cover hair, shoulders and knees; men should avoid sleeveless tops and very short shorts.

A light scarf and comfortable shoes solve most practical issues.

Should You Add Basilica Cistern?

Yes, if you have at least half a day. Basilica Cistern is close, atmospheric and works well when mosque access is paused.

For many travelers, Hagia Sophia plus Blue Mosque plus Basilica Cistern is the strongest first Sultanahmet route.

Sources And Update Note

This guide was updated in July 2026 using the official ticket tariff, current visitor guidance and HagiaSophiaTicket editorial checks. Prayer arrangements, religious holidays, product availability and restoration work can cause same-day changes.

FAQ

Can I visit Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque on the same day?

Yes, they are only a few minutes apart on foot in Sultanahmet Square.

Which should I visit first?

Visit Hagia Sophia first if it is your main priority, especially early in the morning.

Is the Blue Mosque free?

The Blue Mosque is generally free to enter, but visitor access is managed around prayer times.

Can I add Basilica Cistern?

Yes, Basilica Cistern is one of the easiest nearby add-ons and pairs well with both landmarks.

Final Thoughts

The best Hagia Sophia visit is not only about arriving at the building. It is about choosing the right route, timing your visit well and understanding which parts of the experience tickets can and cannot simplify.
