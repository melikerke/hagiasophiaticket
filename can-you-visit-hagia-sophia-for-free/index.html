Can You Visit Hagia Sophia For Free?

⏱ 4 min read✦ Local expert guideUpdated 2026

Hagia Sophia has separate worship and cultural-visitor routes. Worship access to the ground-floor prayer area is free, while sightseeing visitors use the paid upper-gallery route.

Quick answer

As of June 2026, the standard upper-gallery ticket is €25. Children under 8 enter free with a passport or ID. The ground floor is reserved for worship rather than the tourist route.

Prayer Areas

The ground-floor prayer area is reserved for worship. Worship access is free, but it is not the standard cultural sightseeing route for foreign visitors.

Tourist Areas

Cultural visitors enter through a separate tourist entrance and follow the paid upper-gallery route. This route includes elevated interior views and access to important Byzantine mosaics.

What Does A Visitor Ticket Cover?

The standard on-site upper-gallery ticket costs €25 as of June 2026. Children under 8 enter free when an adult presents the child's passport or ID.

Is The Museum Pass Valid?

Museum Pass Istanbul does not cover the standard foreign-visitor upper-gallery admission. Türkiye-issued MüzeKart arrangements are separate and may use a discounted local tariff, so the two products should not be confused.

Why The Confusion Exists

Many online articles contain outdated information. Hagia Sophia's visitor rules have changed multiple times in recent years, so older blog posts may no longer be accurate.

When Free Access Applies

Children under 8 can use the upper-gallery visitor route free with passport or ID. Worship access to the ground floor is free, but tourist sightseeing and worship access are not interchangeable.

Local Tip

Local tip. Use information dated 2026 and distinguish the tourist upper-gallery entrance from the worship entrance. Friday tourist access pauses from approximately 12:00 PM to 2:30 PM.
