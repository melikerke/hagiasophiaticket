Where To Stay In Istanbul: Best Areas For First-Time Visitors

⏱ 7 min read✦ Local expert guideUpdated 2026

Choosing where to stay in Istanbul can feel overwhelming. The city is enormous, it stretches across two continents, and every neighbourhood offers a completely different experience. The good news? Most visitors only need to choose between a handful of areas, and the best neighbourhood for you depends on the kind of trip you're planning.

Best area for first-time visitors: Sultanahmet

If this is your first trip, Sultanahmet is usually the safest choice. This is where you'll find Hagia Sophia, the Blue Mosque, Topkapi Palace, the Basilica Cistern and the Hippodrome. Most major attractions are within walking distance — you can wake up and be standing in front of Hagia Sophia within minutes.

Pros: walk to major attractions, historic atmosphere, easy sightseeing and excellent public transport. Cons: fewer nightlife options, restaurants can be touristy, and it's quieter after sunset. Best for: first-time visitors, families, history lovers and travellers with limited time.

Best area for restaurants & nightlife: Karakoy

Karakoy has changed dramatically over the last decade. Today it combines historic buildings, trendy cafes, cocktail bars, restaurants and waterfront views, and it's very well connected to Sultanahmet, Galata, the Bosphorus and the ferries. Pros: great food scene, central location, modern atmosphere, good transport. Cons: busier than Sultanahmet and can feel crowded.

Best area for couples: Galata

Galata is one of Istanbul's most romantic districts — think narrow streets, boutique hotels, historic buildings and rooftop restaurants, all beneath the famous Galata Tower. At sunset the atmosphere becomes especially magical. Many boutique hotels here offer Bosphorus views, rooftop terraces and historic architecture, perfect for a romantic city break.

Best area for local atmosphere: Cihangir

If you want to feel like you're living in Istanbul rather than simply visiting it, consider Cihangir. This neighbourhood is known for independent cafes, bookstores, artists and local residents, and it feels much more residential than Sultanahmet. Many Turkish actors, writers and creatives spend time here, so the atmosphere feels distinctly local.

Best area for food lovers: Kadikoy

Most tourists never stay on the Asian side — that's a mistake. Kadikoy offers incredible restaurants, markets, local culture and lower tourist density, and the ferry connection to Europe is easy and scenic. You'll find traditional bakeries, fish markets, street food and specialty coffee.

Best area for luxury hotels: Besiktas

Luxury travellers often prefer Besiktas, especially around Dolmabahce Palace, the Bosphorus waterfront and Ortakoy. Many of Istanbul's best hotels are here, with five-star properties, Bosphorus views, fine dining and premium services.

Areas most first-time visitors should avoid

Not because they're unsafe — simply because they're less convenient. Far airport hotels save money but create more problems than they solve, as you'll spend significant time commuting every day. Business districts like Levent and Maslak are important commercial centres but not ideal sightseeing bases.

Sultanahmet or Galata?

Choose Sultanahmet if it's your first visit, you prioritise sightseeing and you want to walk to attractions. Choose Galata if you prefer nightlife, you enjoy cafes and you've already visited Istanbul before.

Karakoy or Kadikoy?

Karakoy is closer to the attractions, more convenient and more tourist-friendly. Kadikoy is more local, has a better food scene and is less crowded with tourists.

How many nights should you stay?

Minimum 3 nights, recommended 4–5 nights, ideal 7+ nights. Many visitors discover Istanbul is much larger than expected.

Local tip from Istanbul. Don't choose a hotel solely because it has a Bosphorus view. A great location often matters more — you might admire the view for ten minutes, but you'll use the location every single day.

FAQ

Is Sultanahmet too touristy?

A little, yes. But it's still the best choice for many first-time visitors.

Is Kadikoy safe?

Yes. It's one of Istanbul's most popular residential areas.

Should I stay on the Asian side?

If you've visited Istanbul before, absolutely.

Which area has the best restaurants?

Kadikoy and Karakoy are both excellent.

What is the most beautiful neighbourhood?

Many visitors would say Galata or Cihangir.

Final verdict

For your first trip, stay in Sultanahmet. For your second trip, stay in Galata, Karakoy or Kadikoy. The best neighbourhood depends on what kind of Istanbul you want to experience — but wherever you stay, one thing becomes clear very quickly: Istanbul isn't one city. It's many cities living side by side.
