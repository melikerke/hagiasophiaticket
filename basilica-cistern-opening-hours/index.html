Basilica Cistern Opening Hours 2026

The Basilica Cistern is easier to plan than Hagia Sophia because prayer times do not affect access, but the daytime and Night Shift ticket windows still matter.

Quick answer

The official visitor page lists Basilica Cistern as open every day from 09:00 to 22:00. Daytime tickets are listed for 09:00-18:30, while Night Shift runs 19:30-22:00 with a separate ticket.

Best time to visit

Morning is usually the safest choice if you want fewer people. If you are visiting Hagia Sophia first, the Basilica Cistern also works well after lunch because the underground space is cooler and does not depend on daylight.

How long does it take?

Allow 30 to 60 minutes for most visits. If you book a guided tour, do not rush: the guide helps explain why this underground water chamber mattered to Byzantine Constantinople.

Same-day route with Hagia Sophia

A simple route is Hagia Sophia first, coffee or lunch around Sultanahmet, then Basilica Cistern. This avoids trying to do two ticketed interiors back to back while hungry or overheated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Basilica Cistern open every day?

The official page lists it as open every day.

What time does Basilica Cistern close?

The overall official opening window is listed until 22:00.

Is Night Shift the same ticket?

No. The official information lists a separate Night Shift price.

Should I visit before or after Hagia Sophia?

Both work, but many visitors prefer Hagia Sophia first and Basilica Cistern afterward.
